📖 Background

Re-identification is the process of matching anonymized data back to specific individuals. Even when direct identifiers (names, emails, SSNs) are removed, combinations of quasi-identifiers can uniquely identify people.

Key Concept: Quasi-identifiers

Attributes that are not unique on their own but become identifying when combined: age, gender, ZIP code, job title, location, dates, etc.

Fact: Research shows that 87% of the US population can be uniquely identified by just ZIP code + date of birth + gender (Sweeney, 2000).

📝 Key Takeaways

  • Removing direct identifiers is NOT sufficient for anonymization
  • Quasi-identifier combinations create fingerprints that can be cross-referenced
  • Small populations (rare jobs, small towns) dramatically increase risk
  • Hashing with predictable inputs is trivially reversible
  • High-value or unusual transactions are inherently identifying
  • Always consider what external data an attacker might have access to
